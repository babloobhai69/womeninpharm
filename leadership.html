Leadership & Professional Development

Leadership and Professional Development is dedicated to supporting women in pharmacy as they explore, build, and advance their academic and professional journeys across healthcare systems and public health sectors. As the role of pharmacy continues to expand beyond traditional practice, women leaders are essential in shaping ethical, evidence-based, and inclusive healthcare environments.

Our initiative provides structured opportunities for mentorship, skill development, and career guidance, enabling women to strengthen their professional identity, leadership confidence, and long-term career clarity. We aim to create a supportive ecosystem where women can grow not only as practitioners, but also as educators, researchers, administrators, and policy contributors.

Mentorship & Guidance

Connect with experienced mentors across academia, clinical practice, research, and public health. Structured mentorship supports informed decision-making, academic growth, and professional confidence.

Career Development Support

Access guidance on career pathways in pharmacy education, healthcare systems, research, industry, and public health. Resources include resume review, interview preparation, and professional positioning.

Leadership Skill Building

Workshops and learning sessions focused on communication, strategic thinking, ethical leadership, collaboration, and professional impact within healthcare and public health environments.

Why Leadership Matters

Strengthening leadership among women in pharmacy enhances representation in decision-making spaces, promotes gender equity in healthcare systems, and contributes to stronger public health outcomes. Leadership development is not only about career advancement—it is about creating influence, driving innovation, and shaping the future of healthcare delivery.

By fostering professional growth and leadership confidence, Women in Pharma aims to cultivate a generation of women leaders who can contribute meaningfully to healthcare reform, policy dialogue, academic excellence, and community health impact.
